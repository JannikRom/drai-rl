\section{Simple Environment Diagnostics}
This appendix presents additional training diagnostics and hyperparameter settings for each evaluated environment. Training curves illustrate reward progression, loss, and the \ac{SAC} specific entropy temperature.
\subsection{Training Curves}
\label{app:simple_losses}
\subsubsection*{Pendulum}
Figure \ref{fig:pendulum_losses} shows additional training diagnostics for the Pendulum environment. All algorithms converge with respect to reward, actor loss, and critic loss, while SAC typically converges slightly faster. The SAC entropy temperature $\alpha$ behaves similarly for \ac{PER} and \ac{ER} variants and decreases over time, reflecting increasing policy confidence.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/overview_pendulum.pdf}
\caption{Training diagnostics for Pendulum environment. 
    Top left: average episode reward. Top right: SAC entropy temperature $\alpha$. 
    Bottom left: actor loss. Bottom right: critic loss.}
\label{fig:pendulum_losses}
\end{figure}

\newpage

\subsubsection*{Lunar Lander}
Training diagnostics for Lunar Lander are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:lunarlander_losses}. Both algorithms exhibit similar reward convergence behavior. The \ac{SAC} actor loss decreases faster than \ac{TD3}, while \ac{TD3} with \ac{PER} remains high for a longer period during training. The entropy temperature for \ac{PER} remains noticeably higher compared to \ac{ER}, indicating increased exploration pressure throughout training.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/overview_lunar_lander.pdf}
\caption{Training diagnostics for Lunar Lander environment. 
    Top left: average episode reward. Top right: SAC entropy temperature $\alpha$. 
    Bottom left: actor loss. Bottom right: critic loss.}
\label{fig:lunarlander_losses}
\end{figure}

\newpage

\subsubsection*{Half Cheetah}
Training diagnostics for Half Cheetah are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:halfcheetah_losses}. All methods converge more slowly compared to the other environments. The highest rewards are achieved by \ac{SAC} with \ac{PER}, while \ac{TD3} achieves comparable performance to \ac{SAC} with \ac{ER}. However, \ac{TD3} with \ac{ER} remains significantly lower in terms of final reward.

Actor losses behave similarly across most configurations, except for \ac{TD3} with \ac{ER}, which exhibits a noticeably higher loss. For the critic loss, the \ac{TD3} \ac{ER} variant again shows lower values compared to the other methods, while \ac{SAC} with \ac{ER} is slightly noisier. Regarding the entropy temperature $\alpha$, \ac{PER} variants require higher values. First, $\alpha$  decreases, then increases, before eventually converging.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/overview_half_cheetah.pdf}
\caption{Training diagnostics for Half Cheetah environment. 
    Top left: average episode reward. Top right: SAC entropy temperature $\alpha$. 
    Bottom left: actor loss. Bottom right: critic loss.}
\label{fig:halfcheetah_losses}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Training Curves}
\label{app:hockey_curves}

Further training diagnostics are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:hockey_losses}. The SAC 
entropy temperature $\alpha$ decays steadily for both variants, reflecting increasing 
policy confidence. The TD3 actor losses decrease monotonically into negative territory 
as the actor learns to maximize the critic's Q-values. The critic loss of TD3 Basic 
appears noisier than the other variants, which may be attributable to the uncorrelated 
white noise producing more inconsistent transition patterns in the replay buffer. 
Critic losses decrease across all variants towards the end of training, indicating 
stable convergence.
