\section{Simple Environment Diagnostics}
This appendix presents additional training diagnostics and hyperparameter settings for each evaluated simple environment. 
Training curves illustrate reward progression, loss, and the \ac{SAC} specific entropy temperature.

\subsection{Training Curves}
\label{app:simple_losses}
\subsubsection*{Pendulum}
Figure \ref{fig:pendulum_losses} shows additional training diagnostics for the Pendulum environment. 
All algorithms converge with respect to reward, actor loss, and critic loss, 
while \ac{SAC} typically converges slightly faster. 
The \ac{SAC} entropy temperature $\alpha$ behaves similarly for \ac{PER} and \ac{ER} variants 
and decreases over time, reflecting increasing policy confidence.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/overview_pendulum.pdf}
\caption{Training diagnostics for Pendulum environment. 
    Top left: average episode reward. Top right: \ac{SAC} entropy temperature $\alpha$. 
    Bottom left: actor loss. Bottom right: critic loss.}
\label{fig:pendulum_losses}
\end{figure}

\newpage

\subsubsection*{Lunar Lander}
Training diagnostics for Lunar Lander are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:lunarlander_losses}. 
Both algorithms exhibit similar reward convergence behavior. 
The \ac{SAC} actor loss decreases faster than \ac{TD3}, while \ac{TD3} with \ac{PER} remains high for a longer period during training. 
The entropy temperature for \ac{PER} remains noticeably higher compared to \ac{ER}, indicating increased exploration pressure throughout training.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/overview_lunar_lander.pdf}
\caption{Training diagnostics for Lunar Lander environment. 
    Top left: average episode reward. Top right: \ac{SAC} entropy temperature $\alpha$. 
    Bottom left: actor loss. Bottom right: critic loss.}
\label{fig:lunarlander_losses}
\end{figure}

\newpage

\subsubsection*{Half Cheetah}
Training diagnostics for Half Cheetah are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:halfcheetah_losses}. 
All methods converge more slowly compared to the other environments. 
The highest rewards are achieved by \ac{SAC} with \ac{PER}, while \ac{TD3} achieves comparable 
performance to \ac{SAC} with \ac{ER}. However, \ac{TD3} with \ac{ER} remains significantly lower in terms of final reward.

Actor losses behave similarly across most configurations, except for \ac{TD3} with \ac{ER}, which 
exhibits a noticeably higher loss. For the critic loss, the \ac{TD3} \ac{ER} variant again shows 
lower values compared to the other methods, while \ac{SAC} with \ac{ER} is slightly noisier. 
Regarding the entropy temperature $\alpha$, \ac{PER} variants require higher values. First, $\alpha$  decreases, then increases, before eventually converging.

\caption{Training diagnostics for Half Cheetah environment. 
    Top left: average episode reward. Top right: \ac{SAC} entropy temperature $\alpha$. 
    Bottom left: actor loss. Bottom right: critic loss.}
\label{fig:halfcheetah_losses}
\end{figure}

tournament results for the hockey environment experiments described in 
Section~\ref{sec:eval}.

\subsection{Training Curves}
\label{app:hockey_curves}

Further training diagnostics are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:hockey_losses}. The \ac{SAC} 
entropy temperature $\alpha$ decays steadily for both variants, reflecting increasing 
policy confidence. The \ac{TD3} actor losses decrease monotonically into negative territory 
as the actor learns to maximize the critic's Q-values. The critic loss of \ac{TD3} Basic 
appears noisier than the other variants, which may be attributable to the uncorrelated 
white noise producing more inconsistent transition patterns in the replay buffer. 
Critic losses decrease across all variants towards the end of training, indicating 
stable convergence.

\subsection{Tournament Results}

The tournament was conducted as a full round-robin in which every participant played 
1000 episodes against every other participant, once as the left and once as the right 
player. Rankings are determined by the TrueSkill lower confidence bound 
$\text{LCB} = \mu - 3\sigma$, where $\mu$ denotes the estimated skill and $\sigma$ 
the uncertainty.

\medskip
Both agents fine-tuned against the pre-trained opponent pool rank first and second by 
a clear margin, confirming the effectiveness of diverse opponent training. It is worth 
noting that all agents ranked below the fine-tuned variants were part of the pre-trained 
opponent pool during their training, meaning the top agents were explicitly optimized 
to beat them. Notably, \ac{SAC} trained only against the weak and strong opponent still 
outperforms \ac{TD3} self-play, suggesting that \ac{TD3} self-play overfits to the strategies 
present in its own snapshot pool. All learned agents reliably surpass both basic opponents, which rank at the bottom of the leaderboard.
